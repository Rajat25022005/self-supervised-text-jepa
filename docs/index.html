// Research Insights
01
Masked Reconstruction Quality: 89.8% vs −19%. This is the headline finding. T-JEPA's objective explicitly trains masked-span prediction in latent space — DistilBERT's MLM does not. A negative score confirms DistilBERT has zero ability to reconstruct masked content in embedding space. This is the metric most faithful to the JEPA training objective.
02
T-JEPA projected (256-dim) beats T-JEPA raw (768-dim) on both discriminability and reconstruction quality — confirming the projection head is learning meaningful latent structure, not just compressing. The JEPA training signal lives in the projected space, as designed.
03
DistilBERT wins on retrieval, few-shot, and MLP probe. Expected — pretrained on 16 GB (BookCorpus + Wikipedia) vs T-JEPA's ~500 MB WikiText-103. A 30× data gap fully explains the classification gap. The objective is not at fault — it's a compute and data story.
04
Next: train to 200k steps on full Wikipedia. Recall@1 trajectory (0.56 at 50k) suggests parity with DistilBERT is achievable with scale. The architecture is validated — it just needs more compute.
